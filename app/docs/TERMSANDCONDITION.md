6.1 BUSINESS REGISTRATION (DO IMMEDIATELY)
Step 1: Register the Company
You have two main options:
Option A — Business Name
Examples:
OsCompanyFinder
OsBusinessFinder
Pros:
Cheaper
Faster
Cons:
Less attractive to enterprise customers
Less attractive to investors

Option B — Limited Liability Company (Recommended)
Examples:
OsCompanyFinder Technologies Ltd
OsCompanyFinder Solutions Ltd
Os Business Intelligence Ltd
Pros:
Professional image
Better for corporate contracts
Easier to raise investment
Better protection between personal and business liabilities
For your long-term vision, I would strongly recommend a Limited Liability Company.

Step 2: Obtain TIN
TIN = Tax Identification Number
You will need it for:
Corporate bank account
Large contracts
Government-related transactions
Tax compliance

Step 3: Open Corporate Account
Never receive company money into a personal account once the business is operational.
Use:
Company name
Company invoice
Company account
This creates trust with enterprise clients.

This document explains:
what users can do
what users cannot do
your responsibilities
their responsibilities

Prohibited Activities
Users must not:

Usage Limits

6.3 DATA USAGE POLICY
This is one of the most important parts of your business.

What Data You Collect
You should clearly state that you may collect:
company name
company website
business phone numbers
business email addresses
company address
publicly available business information

What Data You Do NOT Intentionally Collect
You should state that the platform is not intended to collect:
bank account details
passwords
private communications
medical information
government identification data

Public Data Principle
Your platform should focus on:
publicly available business information
This is much safer than targeting personal information.

Data Source Disclosure
You should disclose that information may come from:
public websites
business directories
publicly available company listings
user-submitted information

Accuracy Disclaimer
Very important.
You should state:
Information provided through the platform may change over time and is not guaranteed to be accurate, complete, or current.
This protects you from claims if data becomes outdated.

6.8 INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP) PROTECTION
Your software itself is valuable.

What You Own
You should claim ownership over:
source code

database design
UI design
logos

What Clients Receive
Clients receive:

Example

You still own:

What you cannot do
A Business Name is different from a Limited Liability Company (Ltd).
For example:
Business Name
OsCompanyFinder Technologies
Limited Company
OsCompanyFinder Technologies Ltd
With a Business Name:
The business and owner are not legally separate.
You have less liability protection.
Some larger corporate clients may prefer dealing with a Ltd company.

Is a Business Name enough for now?
For your current stage, probably yes.
Your immediate goal is:
Build the platform
Get paying customers
Prove the business model
Generate revenue
A Business Name can handle all of that.
Many Nigerian businesses start as Business Names and later convert to a Ltd company once revenue grows.
